H4J



We are Hackers 4 Justice.
Our volunteer-led organization of ethical hackers, DFIR & OSINT specialists, and software developers aid law enforcement in the investigation and elimination of cyber-related threats.

Who We Are

Our mission is to uphold justice, protect the vulnerable, and assist law enforcement agencies through cyber activities that are performed to the highest ethical and legal standards.

DFIR & OSINT Specialists

Assist with collecting open-source intelligence (OSINT), provide DFIR technical support, share cutting-edge techniques and our in-house research material to aid successful investigation.

Ethical Hackers

Our team of experienced and certified cyber security specialists aid and consult with law enforcement across a wide domain of threats. We provide free consultancy on common problematic technologies, such as Tor-based hidden services and various online platforms.

Software Developers

Develop bespoke tools to assist a wide variety of law enforcement operations.

Frequently Asked Questions

I am law enforcement seeking assistance. How can I connect with you?

Using your agency provided email address, you may send an email to info@hackers4justice.com

My agency will require identity verification. Is your organization prepared to submit these documents?

Yes, our team is composed of law-abiding individuals who are prepared to submit identity documentation upon request in order to actively assist in your case.

Can you ensure that all sensitive information is protected?

We utilize security hardened messaging as well as a defense in depth strategy to ensure all sensitive information and documents are handled using feature-rich encryption.

I'm an ethical hacker, how can I help?

Join our Discord group, and navigate to the Join Us channel. Here you will see information detailing our policies. This is where you may choose to apply for membership.

I want to help, but my skills are still developing. Do you provide training?

Yes. Our organization is developing training opportunities created by internal leading industry professionals, and we welcome resource sharing between users within our Discord community.

Is this legal?

Yes, we use various legal frameworks, such as NDAs, MOUs, Letters of Designation, Limited Access Policies and others to ensure all laws are followed. We do not conduct any operations without judical oversight and approval.

Can anyone join?

Citizens of the following countries are not permitted to apply for membership: Afghanistan, Belarus, China, Cuba, Iran, Lebanon, North Korea, Russia, Syria, Venezuela. Individuals who have been convicted of any of the following crimes are not permitted to apply for membership: Any sexual crime, possession of CSAM material, theft or fraud of any kind.

I am neither law enforcement nor a hacker. Are there other ways to support your cause?

1: Individuals who have an existing relationship with their local law enforcement are highly encouraged to reach out and let them know we are here.
2: Official media sources are welcome to contact us via info@hackers4justice.com with the subject line “Media Outreach”
Thank you for your support.

Case Studies

Explore our successful collaborations and impactful projects.

Project: Dark Web

Involved in a CSAM dark web site take down.

About 98% of reported CSAM involves children under the age of 13, with 30% under 10, including infants and toddlers. The National Center for Missing and Exploited Children (NCMEC) received over 36 million reports of suspected child sexual abuse through its CyberTipline. These reports included over 104 million files related to CSAM.

This is just the US.

Project: OSINT

Advised law enforcement of a novel OSINT method which enabled them to correctly identify the real world ip addresses of a Tor-based illegal site.

The Global Anti-Scam Alliance (GASA) has released its 2024 Global State of Scams report, uncovering staggering insights into the prevalence and impact of scams worldwide. The report reveals the monumental economic and emotional toll scams take on consumers. The most eye-opening finding? Scammers have siphoned away over $1.03 trillion globally in just the past year. Which makes the gathering of OSINT more crucial now than ever before.

Collaboration: Research
